What is Hangul? — 한글이란?

Hangul (한글) is the Korean alphabet, created in 1443 by King Sejong the Great (세종대왕). Here's what makes it amazing:

  • It was scientifically designed — the shapes of consonants are based on the shape your mouth and tongue make when you say them!
  • It's easy to learn — unlike Chinese or Japanese, Hangul can be learned in just a few days. Yes, really!
  • Letters combine into blocks — consonants (자음) and vowels (모음) join together to form one syllable-block (음절).
Fun Fact
King Sejong designed Hangul so that "even a wise man can learn it in one morning, and even a fool can learn it in ten days." It truly is that systematic!

PRONUNCIATION TIPS FOR FILIPINO LEARNERS

The 3 tricky sounds: ㅓ vs ㅗ, and ㅡ vs ㅜ

ㅓ
[eo] — "어"
Open your mouth wide
like saying "uh" (surprise face!)
Like English "butter" → the 'u'
ㅗ
[o] — "오"
Round your lips like you're
blowing a candle
Like Tagalog "o" in "oo-o"

Memory trick: ㅓ looks "leaning left" → your jaw drops. ㅗ has a "stick pointing UP" → lips round up!

ㅡ
[eu] — "으"
Smile slightly and say "uh"
Lips stay flat/wide (no rounding!)
Like the "i" in "sir" with flat lips
ㅜ
[u] — "우"
Round lips forward like
you're saying "ooo!"
Like Tagalog "u" in "hulog"

Memory trick: ㅡ is a flat line (flat lips!). ㅜ has a stick pointing DOWN → lips go down into a round O.

Special Notes

ㅇ (이응) — When it appears at the start of a syllable, it's completely silent (just a placeholder). At the end, it sounds like "ng" (like "sing").

ㄹ (리을) — This is between "r" and "l"! Try flicking your tongue lightly, like a soft "d" or rolled "r". Similar to the "r" in Spanish "pero."

ㄱ, ㄷ, ㅂ — These sound softer (like g, d, b) at the start of a word, and harder (like k, t, p) at the end. Don't stress — your ears will learn naturally!
