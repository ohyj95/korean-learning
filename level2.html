Culture tip — 존댓말 vs. Po/Opo

Korean has a formal speech system called 존댓말 (jondaemal) — just like Tagalog uses po and opo to show respect. The difference? In Korean, the entire verb ending changes!

Filipino (Tagalog)
Add po / opo to any sentence
"Salamat po" → Thank you (respectful)
"Opo" → Yes (respectful to elders)
Korean (한국어)
Change the verb ending: -요 / -ㅂ니다
"감사해" (casual) →
"감사합니다" (formal, with seniors)

Good news: All phrases in this guide use the polite -요 / -ㅂ니다 form — so you're already safe with everyone! Just like saying "po" to anyone older or in authority.

2
Self-Introduction — 자기소개
The magic formula

저는 ___입니다.  Jeo-neun ___ im-ni-da.

Just fill in the blank! "저는" = "I am" (formal). "입니다" = the formal "is/am/are".

What to say Korean Pronunciation English
Your name 저는 마리아입니다 Jeo-neun Ma-ri-a im-ni-da I am Maria.
Nationality 저는 필리핀 사람이에요 Jeo-neun Pil-li-pin sa-ram-i-e-yo I'm Filipino.
Occupation 저는 간호사예요 Jeo-neun gan-ho-sa-ye-yo I'm a nurse.
Occupation 저는 학생이에요 Jeo-neun hak-saeng-i-e-yo I'm a student.
Learning Korean 한국어 배우고 있어요 Han-gu-geo bae-u-go i-sseo-yo I'm learning Korean.
