Your first Korean conversations happen before you even leave the airport. Immigration officers speak quickly and use formal language — but they're used to foreign visitors. Stay calm, answer simply, and you'll be fine.
🛂
Immigration 입국심사
인천국제공항 · Incheon International Airport
Beginner
Officer
여권 주세요. Yeo-gwon ju-se-yo. Passport please. Pasaporte po.
You
네, 여기요. Ne, yeo-gi-yo. Yes, here you go. Oo po, heto po.
Officer
방문 목적이 뭐예요? Bang-mun mok-jeok-i mwo-ye-yo? What is the purpose of your visit? Ano ang layunin ng inyong pagbisita?
You
관광이요. Gwan-gwang-i-yo. Tourism / Sightseeing. Para sa turismo po.
Officer
얼마나 있을 거예요? Eol-ma-na i-sseul geo-ye-yo? How long will you stay? Gaano po katagal kayo mananatili?
You
일주일이요. Il-ju-il-i-yo. One week. Isang linggo po.
Officer
네, 즐거운 여행 되세요! Ne, jeul-geo-un yeo-haeng doe-se-yo! Okay, enjoy your trip! Sige po, masayang biyahe!
Key Phrases
관광이요
gwan-gwang-i-yoTourism — the safest one-word answer
일주일이요
il-ju-il-i-yoOne week — 이주일 (2 weeks), 사흘 (3 days)
여기 있어요
yeo-gi i-sseo-yo"Here it is" — useful when handing anything over
Duration Variations
사흘이요
sa-heul-i-yo
3 days (native Korean)
일주일이요
il-ju-il-i-yo
1 week
이 주일이요
i ju-il-i-yo
2 weeks
한 달이요
han dal-i-yo
1 month
Korean immigration officers are usually efficient and brief. You don't need to volunteer information — answer exactly what's asked, simply. If they switch to English, that's fine too. Having your hotel address written down (in Korean if possible) helps a lot.

Currency Exchange 환전
공항 환전소 · Airport exchange booth
Beginner
You
환전하고 싶어요. 달러를 원으로 바꿔 주세요. Hwan-jeon-ha-go si-peo-yo. Dal-leo-reul won-eu-ro bak-kwo ju-se-yo. I'd like to exchange currency. Please change dollars to won. Gusto ko pong mag-palitan ng pera. Palitan po ang dolyar sa won.
Staff
얼마나 바꾸실 거예요? Eol-ma-na bak-ku-sil geo-ye-yo? How much would you like to exchange? Magkano po ang palitan?
You
이백 달러요. I-baek dal-leo-yo. 200 dollars. Dalawang daang dolyar po.
Staff
이십육만 원입니다. I-sim-nyung-man won-im-ni-da. That's 260,000 won. Dalawang daan animnapung libong won po.
You
감사합니다. Gam-sa-ham-ni-da. Thank you. Salamat po.
Key Phrases
환전하고 싶어요
hwan-jeon-ha-go si-peo-yoI'd like to exchange money
~을/를 ~으로 바꿔 주세요
~eul/reul ~eu-ro bak-kwo ju-se-yoPlease change [X] to [Y]
Airport exchange rates are slightly worse than city rates. Myeongdong (명동) in central Seoul has some of the best exchange rates in Korea — look for the "환전" signs. Many booths will give better rates if you ask 더 좋은 환율 없어요? (Are there no better rates?).
Seoul's public transit is world-class — clean, cheap, and on time. The subway (지하철) covers the entire city, and T-money cards work on buses too. These conversations get you from A to B without getting lost.

Buying a T-money Card 교통카드
편의점 · Convenience store
Beginner
You
티머니 카드 있어요? Ti-meo-ni ka-deu i-sseo-yo? Do you have a T-money card? Mayroon po ba kayong T-money card?
Staff
네, 있어요. 얼마 충전해 드릴까요? Ne, i-sseo-yo. Eol-ma chung-jeon-hae deu-ril-kka-yo? Yes, we do. How much would you like to charge? Oo po, mayroon. Magkano po ang gustong i-load?
You
오만 원 충전해 주세요. O-man won chung-jeon-hae ju-se-yo. Please charge 50,000 won. Mag-load po ng limampung libong won.
Staff
카드값 삼천 원 포함해서 오만 삼천 원이에요. Ka-deu-gap sam-cheon won po-ham-hae-seo o-man sam-cheon won-i-e-yo. Including the card fee of 3,000 won, that's 53,000 won total. Kasama ang bayad sa card na tatlong libong won, limampu't tatlong libong won po.
Key Phrases
충전해 주세요
chung-jeon-hae ju-se-yoPlease charge/load — same verb used for phone data!
포함해서
po-ham-hae-seoIncluding — useful in any price context
T-money cards work on subway, bus, and even some taxis. You can buy and charge them at any GS25, CU, or 7-Eleven convenience store. Always tap in AND tap out on the subway — you'll be charged extra if you forget to tap out!

Taking a Taxi 택시
카카오택시 · Kakao Taxi
Beginner
You
명동으로 가 주세요. Myeong-dong-eu-ro ga ju-se-yo. Please take me to Myeongdong. Pakihatid po ako sa Myeongdong.
Driver
네, 알겠습니다. 어디쪽 입구로 가 드릴까요? Ne, al-get-seum-ni-da. Eo-di-jjok ip-gu-ro ga deu-ril-kka-yo? Yes, understood. Which entrance would you like? Opo, nauunawaan ko. Alin pong entrance ang gusto ninyo?
You
명동역 근처면 돼요. Myeong-dong-yeok geun-cheo-myeon dwae-yo. Near Myeongdong station is fine. Malapit lang po sa Myeongdong station, okay na.
Driver
다 왔어요. 여기서 내려 드릴게요. Da wa-sseo-yo. Yeo-gi-seo nae-ryeo deu-ril-ge-yo. We're here. I'll drop you off here. Nandito na po tayo. Dito ko po kayo ibababa.
You
카드 돼요? Ka-deu dwae-yo? Can I pay by card? Pwede po bang mag-card?
Driver
네, 됩니다. Ne, doem-ni-da. Yes, that's fine. Opo, pwede.
Key Phrases
[장소]로 가 주세요
[jang-so]-ro ga ju-se-yoPlease take me to [place] — the essential taxi phrase
근처면 돼요
geun-cheo-myeon dwae-yoNearby is fine — flexible and polite
Kakao T app (카카오T) is the easiest way to hail taxis in Korea — just type your destination in English and it works. Most Korean taxis accept card payment. Tipping is not customary in Korea — don't feel obligated!
Eating in Korea is one of the best parts of the trip. Restaurants are often loud, fast, and call-button or shout-to-order style. Don't be shy — shouting "여기요!" is completely normal and expected.

Korean BBQ 삼겹살집
고기집 · Meat restaurant
Intermediate
Staff
어서 오세요! 몇 분이세요? Eo-seo o-se-yo! Myeot bun-i-se-yo? Welcome! How many people? Maligayang pagdating! Ilang tao po kayo?
You
두 명이요. Du myeong-i-yo. Two people. Dalawa po kami.
Staff
이쪽으로 앉으세요. 주문은 준비되시면 불러 주세요! I-jjok-eu-ro an-jeu-se-yo. Ju-mun-eun jun-bi-doe-si-myeon bul-leo ju-se-yo! Please sit this way. Call us when you're ready to order! Dito po kayo umupo. Tawagan po ninyo kami kapag handa na kayong mag-order!
You
여기요! 삼겹살 이 인분이랑 된장찌개 하나 주세요. Yeo-gi-yo! Sam-gyeop-sal i in-bun-i-rang doen-jang-jji-gae ha-na ju-se-yo. Excuse me! Two portions of samgyeopsal and one doenjang jjigae please. Hoy! Dalawang serving ng samgyeopsal at isang doenjang jjigae po.
Staff
네! 고기는 직접 구우실 건가요? Ne! Go-gi-neun jik-jeop gu-u-sil geon-ga-yo? Sure! Will you grill the meat yourselves? Opo! Kayo po ba ang mag-iihaw ng karne?
You
구워 주세요! Gu-wo ju-se-yo! Please grill it for us! Ikaw na po ang mag-ihaw!
You
여기요! 소주 한 병이랑 맥주 한 병 더 주세요. Yeo-gi-yo! So-ju han byeong-i-rang maek-ju han byeong deo ju-se-yo. Excuse me! One more bottle of soju and one bottle of beer please. Hoy! Isa pang bote ng soju at isang bote ng beer po.
Key Phrases
여기요!
yeo-gi-yo!Hey! / Excuse me! — shout this to call a server, it's normal
~인분
~in-bun~ serving(s) — 일 인분(1), 이 인분(2), 삼 인분(3)
더 주세요
deo ju-se-yoMore please — add to any order
In Korean BBQ restaurants, shouting "여기요!" is the standard way to call a server — not rude at all. Many restaurants now have call buttons on the table (벨을 눌러 주세요 = please press the bell). Side dishes (반찬) are free and refillable — just ask 반찬 더 주세요!

Staff

You

Key Phrases

Korean shopping is an experience — from sleek mall department stores to chaotic street markets. Prices in most stores are fixed, but traditional markets (시장) welcome bargaining. Knowing a few key phrases unlocks the full experience.

Clothes Shopping 옷가게
홍대 쇼핑 거리 · Hongdae shopping street
Intermediate
Staff
어서 오세요! 찾으시는 거 있으세요? Eo-seo o-se-yo! Cha-jeu-si-neun geo i-seu-se-yo? Welcome! Are you looking for something? Maligayang pagdating! May hinahanap po ba kayo?
You
그냥 볼게요. Geu-nyang bol-ge-yo. Just browsing, thanks. Titingnan ko lang po.
You
저기요, 이거 다른 사이즈 있어요? Jeo-gi-yo, i-geo da-reun sa-i-jeu i-sseo-yo? Excuse me, do you have this in another size? Hoy, mayroon po ba itong ibang sukat?
Staff
어떤 사이즈 찾으세요? Eo-tteon sa-i-jeu cha-jeu-se-yo? What size are you looking for? Anong sukat po ang hinahanap ninyo?
You
라지 있어요? Ra-ji i-sseo-yo? Do you have a large? Mayroon po bang large?
Staff
잠깐만요, 확인해 볼게요. Jam-kkan-man-yo, hwak-in-hae bol-ge-yo. One moment, let me check. Sandali lang po, titingnan ko.
You
입어봐도 돼요? I-beo-bwa-do dwae-yo? May I try it on? Pwede ko po bang suokin?
Staff
네, 탈의실은 저쪽이에요. Ne, ta-ri-sil-eun jeo-jjok-i-e-yo. Yes, the fitting room is over there. Opo, ang fitting room po ay naroon.
Key Phrases
그냥 볼게요
geu-nyang bol-ge-yoJust browsing — say immediately to avoid pushy staff
입어봐도 돼요?
i-beo-bwa-do dwae-yo?May I try it on? — ~봐도 돼요 = may I try doing ~?
다른 색 있어요?
da-reun saek i-sseo-yo?Do you have another color?
Korean sizing runs smaller than Western sizes. Most clothes use free size (프리사이즈) or S/M/L. For shoes, sizes use the European millimeter system — 260 = roughly US men's 8 / women's 9.5. Don't be offended if staff suggest a larger size — it's meant to be helpful, not an insult!

Traditional Market 전통시장
광장시장 / 남대문시장 · Traditional markets
Advanced
You
이거 얼마예요? I-geo eol-ma-ye-yo? How much is this? Magkano po ito?
Vendor
만 오천 원이요. Man o-cheon won-i-yo. 15,000 won. Labinlimang libong won po.
You
좀 깎아 주세요. 만 원에 안 돼요? Jom kka-kka ju-se-yo. Man won-e an dwae-yo? Please give me a discount. Can't you do 10,000 won? Pwede po bang magpababa? Sampung libo na lang?
Vendor
아이고, 그건 너무 싸요. 만 이천 원만 해 드릴게요. A-i-go, geu-geon neo-mu ssa-yo. Man i-cheon won-man hae deu-ril-ge-yo. Oh my, that's too cheap. I'll do 12,000 won for you. Ay nako, masyadong mura iyon. Labindalawang libo na lang.
You
두 개 사면 얼마예요? Du gae sa-myeon eol-ma-ye-yo? How much if I buy two? Magkano po kung dalawa ang bibilhin ko?
Vendor
두 개에 이만 원이요. 특별히 깎아 드리는 거예요! Du gae-e i-man won-i-yo. Teuk-byeol-hi kka-kka deu-ri-neun geo-ye-yo! Two for 20,000 won. I'm giving you a special deal! Dalawa para sa dalawampung libo. Espesyal na diskwento iyan!
Key Phrases
좀 깎아 주세요
jom kka-kka ju-se-yoPlease give me a discount — only in traditional markets!
~에 안 돼요?
~e an dwae-yo?Can't you do it for ~? — polite negotiation
두 개 사면 얼마예요?
du gae sa-myeon eol-ma-ye-yo?If I buy two, how much? — great negotiation tactic
Bargaining (흥정) is expected and welcomed in traditional markets but never in department stores, convenience stores, or regular shops. Famous markets: 광장시장 (Gwangjang, fabric & street food), 남대문시장 (Namdaemun, everything), 동대문 (Dongdaemun, fashion).
Koreans are curious and friendly — especially when you try to speak Korean. Don't be surprised if someone asks your age early on, or compliments your Korean after only two sentences. These conversations open real friendships.

Meeting at a Language Exchange 언어 교환
홍대 카페 · Hongdae café
Intermediate
Korean
안녕하세요! 저는 지수예요. 처음 뵙겠습니다. An-nyeong-ha-se-yo! Jeo-neun Ji-su-ye-yo. Cheo-eum boep-get-seum-ni-da. Hello! I'm Jisu. Nice to meet you for the first time. Kumusta! Ako si Jisu. Ikinagagalak kong makilala kayo.
You
저도 반가워요! 저는 [이름]이에요. 필리핀에서 왔어요. Jeo-do ban-ga-wo-yo! Jeo-neun [i-reum]-i-e-yo. Pil-li-pin-e-seo wa-sseo-yo. Nice to meet you too! I'm [name]. I'm from the Philippines. Nagagalak din akong makilala kayo! Ako si [pangalan]. Taga-Pilipinas ako.
Korean
와, 한국어 잘 하시네요! 얼마나 공부했어요? Wa, han-guk-eo jal ha-si-ne-yo! Eol-ma-na gong-bu-hae-sseo-yo? Wow, your Korean is great! How long have you studied? Wow, magaling ka pong mag-Korean! Gaano na po katagal kang nag-aaral?
You
아직 많이 부족해요. 열심히 공부하고 있어요! A-jik man-i bu-jo-kae-yo. Yeol-sim-hi gong-bu-ha-go i-sseo-yo! I'm still lacking a lot. But I'm studying hard! Kulang pa po ako ng marami. Pero masipag akong nag-aaral!
Korean
실례지만, 나이가 어떻게 돼요? Sil-lye-ji-man, na-i-ga eo-tteo-ke dwae-yo? Excuse me for asking, but how old are you? Pasensya na po, pero ilang taon na po kayo?
You
스물다섯 살이에요. 지수 씨는요? Seu-mul-da-seot sal-i-e-yo. Ji-su ssi-neun-yo? I'm 25. What about you, Jisu? Dalawampu't limang taon na po ako. Ikaw naman, Jisu?
Korean
저도 스물다섯이에요! 동갑이네요. 말 편하게 해요! Jeo-do seu-mul-da-seot-i-e-yo! Dong-gap-i-ne-yo. Mal pyeon-ha-ge hae-yo! I'm 25 too! We're the same age. Let's speak casually! Ako rin ay dalawampu't lima! Magkapantay tayo. Makipag-usap na lang tayo nang walang pormal!
Key Phrases
아직 많이 부족해요
a-jik man-i bu-jo-kae-yoI'm still lacking — the humble response to compliments, Koreans love this!
동갑이에요
dong-gap-i-e-yoWe're the same age — triggers an instant switch to casual speech!

mal pyeon-ha-ge hae-yoLet's speak casually / drop the formalities
Koreans ask age early because it determines how to speak to each other — whether to use formal or casual speech (반말 vs 존댓말). It's not rude, it's practical! If you're the same age (동갑), expect an immediate shift to informal speech. Use 씨 (ssi) after someone's name as a polite honorific — 지수 씨, 민준 씨.

Exchanging Contact Info 연락처 교환
카카오톡 · KakaoTalk
Beginner
Korean
카카오톡 있어요? Ka-ka-o-tok i-sseo-yo? Do you have KakaoTalk? Mayroon po ba kayong KakaoTalk?
You
네, 있어요! 아이디 알려 드릴게요. Ne, i-sseo-yo! A-i-di al-lyeo deu-ril-ge-yo. Yes! I'll tell you my ID. Opo, mayroon! Sasabihin ko po sa inyo ang aking ID.
Korean
나중에 또 만나요! Na-jung-e tto man-na-yo! Let's meet again sometime! Magkita-kita ulit tayo!
You
그래요! 연락해요! Geu-rae-yo! Yeol-lak-hae-yo! Sure! Let's keep in touch! Sige! Mag-uusap tayo!
KakaoTalk (카카오톡) is the dominant messaging app in Korea — essentially everyone uses it. If you're in Korea, download it before you go. Sharing your KakaoTalk ID is more common than phone numbers for new acquaintances.
Filipinos and Koreans share a natural warmth that makes meeting new people easy. The phrase 우리 (uri = we/our) is used very generously in Korean — Koreans say "우리 나라" (our country), "우리 엄마" (our mom, even meaning MY mom), creating an instant sense of togetherness. You'll feel right at home!
Korea is one of the safest countries in the world, but things can go wrong anywhere. Knowing these phrases gives you confidence — and could make a real difference. Emergency numbers: 119 (ambulance/fire) · 112 (police) · 1330 (tourist hotline, English available)

편의점 / 약국 · Pharmacy
Intermediate

Pharmacist

Pharmacist
이 약 드세요. 하루에 세 번, 식후에 드시면 돼요. I yak deu-se-yo. Ha-ru-e se beon, sik-hu-e deu-si-myeon dwae-yo. Take this medicine. Three times a day, after meals. Uminom po kayo nito. Tatlong beses sa isang araw, pagkatapos kumain.
You
증상이 심해지면 병원에 가야 하나요? Jeung-sang-i sim-hae-ji-myeon byeong-won-e ga-ya ha-na-yo? If the symptoms get worse, should I go to the hospital? Kung lumala ang sintomas, dapat po ba akong pumunta sa ospital?
Pharmacist
네, 이틀이 지나도 안 나으면 병원에 가 보세요. Ne, i-teul-i ji-na-do an na-eu-myeon byeong-won-e ga bo-se-yo. Yes, if it doesn't improve after two days, please go to the hospital. Opo, kung hindi gumaling pagkatapos ng dalawang araw, pumunta na po kayo sa ospital.

Korean pharmacies (약국) are excellent and pharmacists are very knowledgeable. Many common medicines are available over the counter. For more serious issues, go to a clinic (의원) or hospital (병원). The tourist helpline 1330 has English-speaking staff available 24/7 — save this number!

Lost Item 분실물
지하철 안내소 · Subway information desk
Advanced
You
지갑을 잃어버렸어요. Ji-gap-eul i-reo-beo-ryeo-sseo-yo. I've lost my wallet. Nawala po ang aking pitaka.
Staff
어디서 잃어버리셨어요? Eo-di-seo i-reo-beo-ri-syeo-sseo-yo? Where did you lose it? Saan po ninyo nawala?
You
아까 지하철 안에서 잃어버린 것 같아요. A-kka ji-ha-cheol a-ne-seo i-reo-beo-rin geot ga-ta-yo. I think I lost it on the subway a little while ago. Sa palagay ko ay nawala ko sa loob ng subway kanina.
Staff
몇 호선이었어요? 어떻게 생겼어요? Myeot ho-seon-i-eo-sseo-yo? Eo-tteo-ke saeng-gyeo-sseo-yo? Which line was it? What does it look like? Anong linya po? Ano po ang hitsura nito?
You
이 호선이었어요. 검은색 가죽 지갑이에요. I ho-seon-i-eo-sseo-yo. Geo-meun-saek ga-juk ji-gap-i-e-yo. It was line 2. It's a black leather wallet. Line 2 po. Itim na katad na pitaka po.
Staff
분실물 센터에 확인해 볼게요. 잠깐만 기다려 주세요. Bun-sil-mul sen-teo-e hwak-in-hae bol-ge-yo. Jam-kkan-man gi-da-ryeo ju-se-yo. Let me check with the lost and found center. Please wait a moment. Titingnan ko sa lost and found center. Sandali lang po kayo maghintay.
Key Phrases
~을/를 잃어버렸어요
~eul/reul i-reo-beo-ryeo-sseo-yoI lost my ~ — replace with any item
어떻게 생겼어요?
eo-tteo-ke saeng-gyeo-sseo-yo?What does it look like? — used to describe people and objects
Korea has an impressively high rate of lost items being returned. If you lose something on the subway, go to the station's 분실물 센터 (lost and found). Many wallets and phones are turned in by strangers. The national tourist helpline 1330 can help you file a report in English.
